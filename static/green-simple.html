The ESG Paradox

High-ESG firms face a fundamental trade-off in monetary policy sensitivity. While they enjoy protection from immediate interest rate increases, they simultaneously suffer heightened vulnerability to forward guidance about future rates.

Target Surprise Protection

ESG investors' "warm glow" utility provides a buffer against immediate rate hikes

Path Surprise Vulnerability

Long-term investment horizons make sustainable firms sensitive to future rate expectations

Key Research Insights

Non-linear ESG Effects

The relationship between ESG scores and monetary policy sensitivity is not linear—there's an optimal "sweet spot" in the second quintile.

Industry Transformation

Financial and real estate sectors became the biggest beneficiaries post-Paris, while energy sectors faced increased transition risks.

Investor Behavior

30% of investors derive "warm glow" utility from ESG investments, creating differential demand elasticities during monetary shocks.
